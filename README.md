# ShopSphere – Customer Retention & Cohort Analysis  

## Project Overview  

This project analyses customer purchasing behaviour within an e-commerce environment to uncover insights into retention, engagement, and customer value.  

Using transactional data, the analysis focuses on identifying patterns in repeat purchasing, segmenting customers based on behavioural metrics, and evaluating retention trends over time.  

The goal is to provide data-driven insights that support improved customer retention strategies and maximise long-term business value.

---

## Business Objective  

The business aims to better understand customer behaviour beyond initial transactions, with a focus on:

- Improving customer retention  
- Identifying high-value customer segments  
- Detecting early signs of churn  
- Supporting targeted marketing and engagement strategies  

---

##  Key Business Questions  

- How often do customers return after their first purchase?  
- Which customer segments contribute the most revenue?  
- At what stage do customers disengage?  
- How does retention vary across different customer cohorts?  
- What strategies can improve long-term customer engagement?  

---

## Approach  

The analysis follows a structured workflow:

1. Data Cleaning & Preparation  
2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)  
3. RFM Feature Engineering (Recency, Frequency, Monetary)  
4. Customer Segmentation  
5. Cohort Retention Analysis  
6. Business Insight Generation  

---

## Tools & Technologies  

- Python (Pandas, NumPy)  
- Data Visualisation (Matplotlib, Seaborn)  
- Jupyter Notebook  

---

##  Key Analysis Components  

### 🔹 Exploratory Data Analysis  
- Purchase frequency distribution  
- Revenue distribution  
- Initial behavioural insights  

### 🔹 RFM Segmentation  
- Recency, Frequency, Monetary metrics  
- Customer scoring and segmentation  
- Identification of high-value and at-risk customers  

### 🔹 Cohort Analysis  
- Customer grouping based on first purchase  
- Retention tracking over time  
- Identification of drop-off trends  

---

## Key Insights  

- Customer behaviour is highly skewed, with a large proportion of customers making few purchases  
- A smaller segment of customers contributes a disproportionate share of revenue  
- Retention declines significantly after the first few periods, indicating early-stage disengagement  
- Distinct customer segments (e.g., loyal, at-risk) highlight opportunities for targeted interventions  

---

## Strategic Recommendations  

- Implement targeted retention campaigns for at-risk customers  
- Develop loyalty programs to strengthen high-value customer relationships  
- Introduce personalised marketing based on customer segments  
- Focus on early engagement strategies to reduce initial drop-off  

---

##  Limitations  

- Dataset limited to transactional data (no demographic or behavioural context)  
- Analysis restricted to a single time period  
- No external factors (e.g., marketing campaigns) included  

---

##  Future Work  

- Build predictive churn models  
- Integrate customer demographic and behavioural data  
- Develop real-time dashboards for monitoring retention  
- Conduct A/B testing on retention strategies
